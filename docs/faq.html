FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Plain English answers to common questions about Blockchella.

What is Sepolia testnet? +

Sepolia is a copy of Ethereum used for testing. It behaves identically to the real Ethereum network, but the ETH on it (called SETH or Sepolia ETH) has no monetary value and is free to obtain from faucets. Developers use testnets to build and test applications without spending real money. Blockchella runs on Sepolia so you can try it risk-free.

What is SETH? +

SETH stands for Sepolia ETH — the native currency of the Sepolia testnet. It works exactly like ETH but is worthless outside the testnet. You use SETH to pay for ticket prices and gas fees on Blockchella. You can get free SETH from faucets like sepoliafaucet.com.

Why do I need MetaMask? +

Blockchella has no user accounts, no passwords, and no central server storing your data. Instead, your Ethereum wallet (MetaMask) is your identity. When you buy a ticket, the transaction is signed by your wallet's private key and recorded permanently on-chain. MetaMask also holds your SETH so you can pay for tickets and gas fees.

What happens if my group deadline expires? +

If not all group members have paid when the deadline passes, the group is considered failed. Anyone can call refundGroup(groupId) to trigger the cleanup. Everyone who paid into escrow gets their full ticket price back automatically. Reserved tickets are released back to the available pool. Note: gas fees from the original payment transactions are not refunded by the contract — those are consumed by the Ethereum network.

Can I scalp or resell my ticket? +

No. The smart contract enforces that ticket tokens can only be transferred back to the venue (contract owner). You cannot send your ticket to another wallet or sell it on a marketplace. The only option is to return it for a full refund via the Return Ticket page. This anti-scalping mechanism is enforced at the code level — no company needs to police it.

What is a smart contract? +

A smart contract is a program that runs on a blockchain. Once deployed, its code cannot be changed (unless it was specifically built to be upgradeable, which Blockchella is not). It runs automatically when someone sends it a transaction, and the rules it enforces cannot be overridden by anyone — not even the contract's creator. Think of it as a vending machine: put in the right input, get the guaranteed output.

Why does my transaction cost gas? +

Gas is a fee paid to Ethereum validators for processing and storing your transaction. Every operation on Ethereum — reading state, writing state, doing arithmetic — costs a small amount of gas. On Sepolia, gas is paid in SETH (worthless test funds), so it costs nothing in real money. On mainnet it would cost real ETH. The gas fee goes to network validators, not to Blockchella.

What is the Blockchella chain? +

Blockchella builds its own mini-blockchain inside the smart contract. Every time a group successfully completes, a new "block" is appended to this internal chain, recording the group ID, member addresses, ticket count, and a hash linking it to the previous block. This creates a tamper-evident audit trail of all completed ticket groups — a blockchain running on top of Ethereum. See the On-Chain Blockchain doc for details.

How do I verify my ticket at the door? +

Door staff use the Verify page (verify.html) to check ticket ownership. As an attendee, you show your wallet address (visible in MetaMask) to the doorman. They enter it on the Verify page, and the contract is queried to confirm whether that address holds a ticket token. No QR codes, no paper tickets — your wallet is your ticket.

Can I return my ticket? +

Yes. Any ticket holder can return their ticket at any time using the Return Ticket page. The ticket token is burned and the full ticket price is refunded in SETH to your wallet. Gas fees are not refunded. Once returned, the ticket count returns to the available pool and someone else can buy it.
